About the Candidate

Meet Mike Pentola

Mike Pentola is a South Shore neighbor focused on practical, people-first leadership. His campaign is rooted in public service, accountability, and results that residents can feel in their daily lives—from cleaner shorelines and safer roads to transparent county budgeting.

Raised in Long Island communities where civic pride runs deep, Mike believes local government works best when it listens first, acts quickly, and reports back honestly. District 4 deserves representation that is present, responsive, and ready to collaborate across every neighborhood.

“Our district is strongest when every block, beach, and business corridor has a voice at the table.”— Mike Pentola
Long Island Native Community Advocate
Voter Information

Know Your Vote

County legislators vote on budgets, public safety, infrastructure, and local laws that directly affect transportation, taxes, and quality-of-life services.

District 4 includes Long Beach, Lido Beach, Island Park, Point Lookout, East Atlantic Beach, Harbor Isle, Barnum Island, and portions of Oceanside.

Election Day is currently set to Tuesday, November 3, 2026. Please verify dates with Nassau County BOE before voting.

You can register through New York State Elections online portal or by submitting a voter registration form by the required deadline.

Yes. New York offers early voting and mail/absentee options for eligible voters. Check current windows and requirements with your election office.

District 4 Priorities

Issues That Matter Most

🌊 Protecting Our Shores & Environment

Protect our beaches, improve water quality, and preserve coastal ecosystems for future generations.

🏠 Property Taxes & Affordability

Advance tax relief and transparent budgeting so families and seniors can afford to stay in District 4.

🛡️ Public Safety

Strengthen partnerships between residents and law enforcement to keep neighborhoods safe and connected.

🏗️ Infrastructure & Flood Resilience

Invest in roads, drainage, and flood mitigation with lessons learned from Sandy and climate impacts.

🏪 Local Businesses & Economy

Cut red tape, support local entrepreneurship, and grow resilient year-round jobs across South Shore communities.

🎓 Quality Schools & Youth Programs

Champion school resources and after-school programs that help kids thrive in safe, enriching environments.
